Goals & Results

Statement of Goals

At the start of the project, we identified the following goals for improving upon the baseline line-following code provided with the PiCar-X API examples:

  • Learn to connect to the PiCar robot Our first goal was simple: we needed to connect to our PiCar robot! Our group accomplished this in many different ways. Initially, progress was slow. We had to connect the robot to a monitor, keyboard and mouse every time we wanted to use it. However, we eventually figured out some workarounds which allowed us to connect to the robot extremely quickly with screen-mirroring software or the raspberry pi operating system. We also figured out how to streamline our development by doing most of our coding on our personal devices, and then doing and SSH into the pi with Cyberduck.
  • Test and understand the hardware we're working with Once we were familiar with the pi operating system, we wanted to test our hardware. To do this, we created several different hardware test files which tested the functionality of the sensor, camera and wheels of the robot.
  • Begin the robot's calibration Our next step was getting the robot ready for our final line-following program. We constructed an early draft of our calibration file, which specifically focused on providing a consistent, reliable basis for our robot to run our future line-following alrogithm.
  • Develop and test a successful line-following program Our last goal was the biggest. In this one, we took all of the knowledge we had gained over the course of the project and creatd a successful line-following program, which recognizes 90-degree turns and intersections.

Results

  • Goal 1: Learn to connect Achieved We learned to effectively connect and use our robot's software
  • Goal 2: Test hardware Achieved We conducted multiple thorough tests of the hardware functionality
  • Goal 3: Calibrate robot Achieved We provided a basic calibration for our robot to run our future programs
  • Goal 4: Implement line following Achieved We completed effective line-following code on our robot which can handle sharp turns and intersections
